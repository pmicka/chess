About

Me vs the World is a slow, social chess experiment: one host versus everyone else sharing a single board. Games unfold over hours or days, relying on patience and clear updates instead of clocks.

The Game

Visitors coordinate moves as a team, while the host responds in turn. The board state and PGN remain public, and the active side alternates between games to keep things fair.

Process

The stack is intentionally simple: PHP and SQLite on the server, chess.js in the browser, and a lightweight UI. No frameworks or build steps are required to play, experiment, or contribute.

Contact

Want to follow along or contribute ideas? Reach out via GitHub or drop a note about what you would like to see next.
